# Report spam, phishing, or malware

If you find information in Google's search results that you believe appears due to spam,
  paid links, malware, or other quality issues, use one of the following forms. While Google does not use these reports
  to take direct action against violations, these reports still play a significant role in
  helping us understand how to improve our spam detection systems that protect our search results.

## Spammy, deceptive, or low quality web pages

Ranking manipulation techniques that attempt to compromise the quality of Google's search
  results violate our [spam policies](/search/docs/essentials/spam-policies)
    and can negatively impact a site's ranking.

[Report spam](https://search.google.com/search-console/report-spam)

## Malware

  If you believe a site is infected with
  [malware or distributing malicious or unwanted software](/search/docs/essentials/spam-policies#malware-and-malicious-behaviors),
  notify us.

[Report malware](https://www.google.com/safebrowsing/report_badware/)

## Phishing

  If you believe you've encountered a page designed to look like another page in an attempt to
  steal users' personal information, complete the form below to report the page to the Google
  Safe Browsing team.

[Report phishing](https://safebrowsing.google.com/safebrowsing/report_phish/)
